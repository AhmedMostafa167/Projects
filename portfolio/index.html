About

I'm Ahmed. Computer Science graduate, currently building NLP & LLM systems with the same engineering discipline I'd want at a real job: typed configs, unit tests, Docker, CI, and one-command deployments. No demo-ware.

My route into AI was unusual. I started in quantum computing — interned at QUAE Research developing an EEG classification pipeline with Quantum Variational Autoencoders, which became a published paper at the Neurogenomics 2024 conference. That experience taught me how to read papers, run rigorous experiments, and ship reproducible work.

Now I focus on what I find most exciting: language. RAG architectures, parameter-efficient fine-tuning, and the engineering glue that turns a clever model into a useful product. Everything you see on this page is deployed and documented end-to-end.

I like working across disciplines — from generative vision and quantum computing to applied NLP — and my coursework reflects that breadth. I've also served as a teaching assistant, leading tutorials in linear algebra, programming, and information retrieval, which sharpened how I explain complex ideas simply.

Research

PUBLISHED · MACHINE GRAPHICS & VISION 2025

Fine-tuning Stable Diffusion for Generating 2D Floor Plans Using Prompt Templates

We fine-tune Stable Diffusion to generate 2D architectural floor plans from structured prompt templates, training on a curated dataset of floor-plan images spanning diverse design styles and layouts. The work pairs generative modelling with prompt engineering to turn natural-language descriptions into usable architectural drafts.

Machine Graphics & Vision, 34(3), 77–95 Read the paper
PUBLISHED · NEUROGENOMICS 2024

Quantum Denoising in the Realm of Brain-Computer Interfaces: A Preliminary Study

We explore how a Quantum Variational Autoencoder can denoise EEG signals before classification, evaluated on a brain-computer-interface task. Published at the Neurogenomics 2024 Conference; my contribution was the EEG classification pipeline and the comparative evaluation against classical baselines.
